Parts of the brain you need to know about before you read on:

Cerebral Cortex

The cerebral cortex is responsible for perceiving, thinking, reasoning and imagining. It is the outer most part of the brain and is, evolutionarily speaking, the newest part of the Brain. It is divided into four lobes (parts), however, I will be mainly focusing on the occipital and parietal lobes.

  1. Occiptal lobe:
    The occipital lobe allows you to see and process stimuli from the external world and assign meaning to and remember visual perceptions. It determines colour properties on the visual field and assesses distance, size and depth. The occipital lobe also transmits visual information to other brain regions, allowing brain lobes to encode memories and assign meaning.

  2. Parietal lobe:
    One of the main function of the parietal lobe is to receive and process sensory information from the body. It works together with other areas of the brain such as the visual cortex to perform certain tasks such as opening a door.

The Cerebellum

The cerebellum is sometimes referred to as the ‘Little Brain’ and receives information from sensory nerves and the auditory and visual systems. It makes up approximately 10% of the brain’s total size but accounts for more than 50% of the total number of neurons located in the brain.

The Thalamus

The Thalamus is responsible for relaying information from sensory receptors to appropriate areas of the brain where it can be processed.
